Facial Recognition Program

Researcher, Programmer

My partner and I developed a program for facial recognition using Eigenfaces and Principle Component Analysis as the underlying techniques for recognition. The essential structure of the program takes an input image an a matrix of numbers, determines the most important components of that image and then reconstructs the image using only those principle components. This reconstructed image is then compared with a set of images to determine images with similar principle components, thereby generating a match. In conjunction with the program itself, we wrote a report detailing the design and success of the Eigenfaces program and comparing its success with other popular facial recognition methods.

Autonomous Ground Robot

Project Lead, Designer

My team and I are developing a fully autonomous ground robot capable of navigating a course complete with stationary and moving obstacles using a combination of LIDAR-based, vision-based, and infrared sensing. The fabricated robot shell was developed in Solidworks using Grabcad and made out of Birch plywood and ABS plastic. All of the robot shell parts were designed and fabricated in-house at Olin using our Shopbot CNC Router and Stratasys ABS printers. The software control system for the robot is developed in C utiliing ROS (robot operating system), with all software autonomy running onboard on an Odroid Xu4.

Play Project: Developing Compassion through Gameplay Experiences

Mechanical Designer

Building off human-centered design techniques, my team and I developed a gameplay experience for children with the intention of teaching compassion and responsibility. The gameplay itself consists of a child assuming the role of a Llama by wearing a helmet with a llama head and spit cannon embedded inside it. The Llama is responsible for protecing stuffed sheep from an RC car "wolf" attacker. Through the experience of gameplay, children are taught to take responsibility for another being and develop compassion and empathy.
