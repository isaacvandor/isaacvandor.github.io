Olin College Remote Sensing and Sampling Lab

Working on contract with NOAA, I am currently performing research into new methods for large pelagics tracking and tagging. This includes the development of a mobile application for recreational fishermen, enabling more efficient and successful methods of fish tagging and population tracking. Part of this research involves a novel method for tracking fish using rfid enabled rice grain tags to store information, which is then sent via bluetooth to our mobile application and automatically uploaded to a centralized database.

Mechanical Design Sculptures

As part of an introductory class in mechanical prototyping, I designed, prototyped, and fabricated two kinematic sculptures. The first kinematic sculpture is powered by a small DC motor while the other sculpture is entirely wind-powered. Numerous design and fabrication techniques were employed in the creation of these sculptures, as they were intended to demonstrate the different structures, transmissions, and fasteners inherent in mechanical design. In addition to the fabrication of these two sculptures, a full CAD package and design report was created highlighting the various fabrication techniques used.

Facial Recognition Program

As part of a class exploring engineering through the framework of quantitative analysis, I developed a program for facial recognition using Eigenfaces and Principle Component Analysis as the underlying techniques for recognition. The essential structure of the program takes an input image, determines the most important, or princple, components of that image by developing a data matrix based on that image and finding the various eigenvectors of that matrix, and then reconstructs the image using only those principle components. This reconstructed image is then compared with a set of images to determine the most similar images, thereby finding a match for the same person. In conjunction with the program itself, I wrote a report detailing the design and success of the Eigenfaces program and comparing its success with another facial recognition program developed using a euclidean distances method.

Boat Hull Design

For a class project exploring multivariable calculus and the physics of sailing, I designed and developed a boat hull, constructed entirely from EPS foam and adhesives. The fabricated boat hull was developed by extrapolating a 3D model for the hull based on equations governing the waterline and cross-sections of a 2-Dimensional model. While the 2D model was developed using Matlab, the design of the boat was developed in Solidworks before being fabricated on a Shopbot. After testing the boat, a report was written analyzing the performance of the design and the various methods for improving the design and fabrication process in future iterations.

Modeling the Israeli Iron Dome Defense System

For the final project in a class on modeling and simulation of the physical world, my partner and I developed a model of the Israeli Iron Dome Missile Defense System, a system meant to defend against ballistic missile attack. In order to model such a system, equations governing the flight of missiles had to be developed in Matlab and variables such as drag, lift, thrust, and others were considered to create a realistic model. Once developed, the data generated by the model was used to optimize energy usage of the Iron Dome missiles thereby generating cost savings as well, an important optimization because of the high cost of deploying an Iron Dome missile.

Play Project: Developing Compassion through Gameplay Experiences

Building off human-centered design techniques taught in an introduction to design class at Olin, my team and I developed a gameplay experience for elementary age children with the intention of teaching compassion and responsibility through the course of the gameplay experience. The gameplay itself consists of the child assuming the role of a Llama by wearing a helmet with a llama head and spit cannon embedded inside it. As the Llama, it is the users responsibility to protect sheep, as this is a common role of llamas on farms. While the user is protecting the sheep, an RC wolf drives through a forest of trees with the intention of stealing a baby sheep. In order to stop the wolf, the llama must shoot "spitballs" at it, to frighten it away. Through the experience of gameplay, children are taught to take responsibility for another being and develop compassion for the sheep.

Pharmacokinetics: Data Modeling and Analysis

For a class dealing with the abstraction of physical systems, my partner and I explored the pharmacokinetics of Warfarin (brand name: Coumadin), a commonly used blood thinner with an extreme variability in dosage and side effects. Specifically, my partner and I developed a set of general dosage recommendations based on pharmacokinetic data of Warfarin taken from multiple studies of patients with varying ages, genders, physical attributes, etc. Historically, dosages of Warfarin have been administered in a trial and error manner whereby a patient begins on an extremely low dose and their dosage is gradually increased until they are taking an effective dosage that does not have adverse effects. However, this method is incredibly dependent on the individual doctor and patient and can lead to dramatic consequences, including the death of the patient. As such, my partner and I developed a set of recommendations intended to provide doctors with a baseline dosage range for prescribing dosages of Warfarin to patients of varying ages.
